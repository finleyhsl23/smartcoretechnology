Cold Call Script

Word-for-word — from first ring to booked demo

This is a guide, not a teleprompter. Know it well enough that it sounds like you. The goal of every call is one thing: get 15 minutes on a screen share.

1
📞 Gatekeeper
You — Dylan

"Hi there — it's Dylan calling from SmartCore Technology. Could you put me through to whoever looks after your sales or customer management systems, please?"

💡
Don't over-explain. A confident, specific request ("whoever looks after your sales systems") bypasses the "what's it about?" deflection better than "I'd like to speak to your manager." If they ask, say it's about your CRM platform — keep it brief.
2
🎯 Decision Maker — Opening
You — Dylan

"Hi — is that the [sales manager / director]? My name's Dylan from SmartCore Technology. Have you got 30 seconds? I promise I'll be brief."

💡
Asking permission resets the dynamic — they've agreed to hear you rather than being talked at. Keep your energy calm and conversational, not salesy. If they say "not right now," ask when would be a better time and lock in a specific call-back.
→ If they say yes
You

"Brilliant — I'll be quick. The reason I'm calling is that we've built a CRM platform — here in the UK — that helps growing businesses manage their leads, customers, and sales pipeline in one place."

3
🎯 The 30-Second Pitch
You — Dylan

"It covers everything in one system — quotes with online signing, a customer portal, AI-powered automations, follow-up reminders, and sales reporting. The idea is your team spends less time on admin and more time actually winning business."

You — pause, then

"The reason I'm reaching out specifically is that a lot of businesses we speak to are running on a mix of spreadsheets and a CRM that doesn't quite do everything. I was just wondering — what are you currently using to manage your customer relationships and pipeline?"

💡
Stop talking and let them answer. This is the most important moment of the call — whatever they say next tells you exactly which angle to take. Don't fill the silence.
4
🔍 Discovery — After They Answer
→ If they name a tool (HubSpot, Salesforce, spreadsheets, etc.)
You

"Right, that makes sense — [tool] is a common one. The question I'd ask is whether you're actually getting full use out of it, or paying for features you don't need. A lot of the businesses we work with find that by the time they add quotes, e-signature, and a proper customer-facing portal, the cost adds up significantly."

→ If they say spreadsheets / email / "nothing formal"
You

"That's actually really common — and it tends to work right up until it doesn't. The moment you're losing track of a follow-up or a quote hasn't been sent, it starts costing you deals. That's exactly the kind of thing SmartCore is designed to fix."

5
✅ The Ask — Book the Demo
You — Dylan

"Would you be open to a quick 15-minute screen share sometime next week? No obligation — I'll just show you the system running with your kind of business in mind, and you can decide within five minutes whether it's worth a closer look."

💡
"15 minutes" and "no obligation" lower the barrier. If they say yes, lock in a specific day and time immediately — don't say "I'll send a link." Send a calendar invite before you hang up.
→ "Just send me some information instead"
You

"Absolutely — I'll send something over. But honestly, it's one of those things that's much clearer when you see it live than reading about it. Can I send you a link and we book 15 minutes? If it's not relevant after 5 minutes, I'll let you go. Fair enough?"

→ "We're happy with what we have"
You

"That's fair — and I'm not suggesting you rip everything out. It's more just a question of whether what you have is doing everything you need it to, or whether there are gaps costing you time. Five minutes on a screen share is usually enough to tell. Worth a look?"

✓ Do
Ask for permission before pitching
Use their company name if you have it
Stop talking after you ask a question
Name a specific day when booking the demo
Sound like you're calling a colleague, not reading a script
Follow up with a calendar invite the same day

The Sales Script

What to say, and when to say it

Use this as a conversation guide, not a script to read verbatim. The goal is to get them talking about their current pain, then show them how SmartCore solves it specifically.

Opening line — first 60 seconds

"Most businesses we work with are running on a combination of spreadsheets, email, and maybe a basic CRM that doesn't really talk to anything else. The problem isn't just the chaos — it's that nothing is visible. Your leads are in one place, your quotes are in your inbox, your support tickets are in your head. SmartCore brings all of that under one roof, and because it's built by us specifically, we can adapt it to the way you work — not the other way around."

Discovery questions to ask first

Find their pain before pitching

  • How are you currently tracking your leads and follow-ups?
  • When a new enquiry comes in, what happens to it?
  • Do you have visibility of what's in your pipeline right now?
  • How do you send quotes — are they consistent, professional-looking?
  • How long does it take from quote to signed agreement?
  • When a customer has an issue, how do they reach you?
  • Do you have any idea which of your team is performing best?
Common pain points we solve

Listen for these triggers

  • "We lose track of enquiries" → Lead pipeline & reminders
  • "Quotes take too long to get out" → Quote builder + e-signature
  • "Customers chase us for updates" → Customer portal
  • "I don't know what my team is doing" → Tasks, calendar, leaderboard
  • "We're spending a fortune on Salesforce" → Price comparison
  • "Support requests come in from everywhere" → Tickets + messaging
  • "We want to send newsletters / offers" → Newsletter tool
Handling Objections
🤔

"We already have a CRM — HubSpot / Salesforce / Monday."

💬

"That's great — those are well-known tools. The question is whether you're actually using all of it, and whether you're paying for features you don't need. HubSpot's CRM starts free but the moment you want automation, quotes, or a proper portal, you're looking at hundreds a month — sometimes over a thousand. SmartCore gives you a complete system from £19.99, with e-signatures, a customer portal, and AI baked in. And if you want anything custom, you deal with a real UK team, not a support ticket that takes 3 days."

🤔

"It seems quite small — is it enterprise-grade?"

💬

"Enterprise-grade doesn't mean complicated — it means reliable, secure, and scalable. SmartCore runs on Cloudflare's global edge network and Supabase (PostgreSQL) — the same infrastructure used by companies processing millions of requests a day. Being a smaller provider works in your favour: you're not a ticket number, and changes that would take Salesforce 6 months to configure take us days."

🤔

"What if we need something specific that you don't have?"

💬

"That's exactly the conversation we want to have. Unlike off-the-shelf CRMs, SmartCore is built to be extended. Tell us what you need — a specific workflow, a custom report, an integration — and we can discuss adding it. We built an AI chat assistant, e-signature flow, and leaderboard for our existing customers because they asked for it. Your feedback shapes the product."

🤔

"We're worried about data security / GDPR."

💬

"Absolutely fair concern. Your data is stored in isolated database schemas — your records are never mixed with another company's. All traffic runs over HTTPS with enterprise-grade encryption at rest. We're a UK company operating under UK/EU data protection law, and we can provide a data processing agreement on request. We don't sell data, and we never have."

🤔

"Can we try it before we commit?"

💬

"Yes — we can get a demo environment set up with your branding and some dummy data so you can actually click around in something that feels like yours. The best way to sell it is to show it, not describe it. Can we book 30 minutes this week?"
